{{ title }} Rotary evaporator (rotavap)

Our Rotary apparatus is ideal for effective removal of solvent from sample using evaporation. Rotary evaporators are versatile tools used in a variety of applications beyond continuous distillation of volatile solvents. They play a key role in concentration, crystallization, drying, separation, and solvent recovery processes. These instruments are widely utilized across numerous industries, such as pharmaceuticals, chemicals, and biotechnology.

{{ subtitle }} Working Principle Of Rotary Apparatus


Rotary evaporators operate based on the principle that solvents have different boiling points, which can be lowered under reduced pressure. A rotary evaporator works on the principle that as the vacuum in the evaporation flask increases, the boiling point of the liquid inside lowers. The evaporation flask spins at a designated speed, creating a thin, uniform film of material on the inner surface of the flask. This maximizes the surface area for efficient evaporation. A rotary evaporator is often linked to a vacuum pump to improve process efficiency. This setup creates a vacuum, which in turn lowers the boiling point of the solution and speeds up evaporation.

{{ subtitle }} Rotary Evaporator Usages


  1. Pharmaceutical Industry: Rotary evaporators are used for drug formulation and synthesis, purification, and concentration of pharmaceutical compounds.
  2. Chemical Industry: They play a crucial role in the synthesis, purification, and concentration of chemicals, including organic and inorganic compounds.

  7. Petroleum Industry: In this industry, rotary evaporators can be used for refining processes and in the study of fuel components.
  8. Environmental Science: Rotary evaporators aid in the analysis and treatment of samples for environmental research, such as water and soil testing.

{{ subtitle }} Operating Procedure


  1. 1. Place your sample in a clean round bottom flask and connect the flask to the condenser.
  2. 2. Use vacuum grease and a Keck clamp if needed for sealing and securing connections.
  3. 3. Check that the collection flask is clean
  4. 4. Make sure the chiller, condenser, and vacuum pump are all properly connected.
  5. 5. Turn on the chiller and set the temperature to the desired level (usually around 11°C, but not below 9°C).
  6. 6. Turn on the vacuum pump and check the gauge for proper vacuum (around 27 in Hg).
  7. 7. Fill the hot bath with water for the round bottom flask to sit in.
  8. 8. Adjust the position of the flask and set the desired temperature on the hot bath.
  9. 9. Turn on the rotary evaporator and set the desired spin speed and/or evaporation time.
  10. 10. Start the evaporation process by pressing the dial on the control panel.
  11. 11. Once evaporation is complete, stop the rotary evaporator by pressing the dial again.
  12. 12. Raise the system, turn off the hot bath, chiller, and vacuum pump.
  13. 13. Vent the system by opening the release valve.
  14. 14. Remove your product and clean all flasks.
